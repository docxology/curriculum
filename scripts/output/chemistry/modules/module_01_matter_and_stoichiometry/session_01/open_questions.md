## Open Question 1: What is the mechanism of microbial dark matter interactions in subsurface aquifers?

Context: Recent deep biosphere research has revealed unexpectedly high microbial diversity in deep subsurface environments. However, a significant portion of this microbial community remains uncharacterized. Understanding how these “dark matter” microbes interact – particularly through electron transfer and nutrient exchange – is critical to accurately modeling global carbon cycling and assessing the potential for subsurface biogenic methane production.  This research aims to illuminate the potential for unseen biogeochemical processes.

Current research:  Geobiochemistry, Microbial Ecology, Systems Biology, Electron Microscopy.

## Open Question 2: How does the quantum entanglement of photons within biological chromophores affect photosynthetic efficiency in algae?

Context: Traditional models of photosynthesis often treat chromophores (pigments) as simple energy absorbers. However, emerging research suggests that quantum phenomena, specifically the entanglement of photons, might play a significant role in energy transfer within these complex molecules.  Determining if and how entanglement impacts the efficiency of light harvesting and energy transport in photosynthetic systems represents a major advance in understanding biological energy conversion.

Current research: Quantum Biology, Biophysics, Spectroscopy, Quantum Computing.

## Open Question 3: What are the implications of engineered retroviruses for targeted gene therapy in complex autoimmune diseases?

Context: While CRISPR-Cas9 offers precision gene editing, its application in treating complex diseases like rheumatoid arthritis and multiple sclerosis faces challenges regarding off-target effects and delivery. Engineered retroviruses – modified to excise their viral DNA – present a potential solution, offering greater control and potentially long-lasting gene correction.  Understanding the safety and efficacy of this approach is crucial for developing novel therapies.

Current research: Synthetic Biology, Gene Therapy, Viral Vector Engineering, Immunotherapy.
