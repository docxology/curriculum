Context: Contrastive Predictive Coding (CPC) has emerged as a powerful technique in reinforcement learning, demonstrating remarkable success in environments with sparse rewards. It leverages the principle of predicting future states based on past experience, effectively “teaching” the agent to anticipate and learn from infrequent signals.  Current research focuses on understanding the theoretical underpinnings and extending CPC to more complex, real-world scenarios, particularly those involving high-dimensional sensory input.

Context: Meta-learning – “learning how to learn” – offers a promising approach to tackling the problem of autonomous exploration.
