## Open Question 2: How does microglial polarization affect the progression of Alzheimer’s disease?

Context: Microglia, the brain’s resident immune cells, play a complex and often contradictory role in Alzheimer's disease. While traditionally viewed as detrimental due to their pro-inflammatory responses, emerging research suggests that microglial polarization – shifting between pro-inflammatory (M1) and anti-inflammatory (M2) states – significantly impacts disease progression. Understanding the specific signals and pathways governing this polarization is a critical area of investigation for developing novel therapeutic strategies. Current research explores the role of specific cytokines and epigenetic modifications.

## Open Question 3: What are the implications of quantum entanglement for secure communication networks?

Context: Quantum entanglement offers the potential for completely secure communication networks, as any attempt to intercept or eavesdrop on entangled particles would fundamentally alter their state, immediately alerting the sender and receiver. While significant technological hurdles remain, the potential impact of harnessing quantum entanglement for secure communication is attracting intense research interest. Current research is heavily focused on generating, maintaining, and transmitting entangled states over long distances, alongside developing quantum key distribution protocols.
