Following grafting, the grafts will be monitored daily for signs of vascular integration, evidenced by the formation of a callous tissue at the graft junction. Statistical analysis (ANOVA and post-hoc tests) will be used to determine significant differences in the success rates among the different cleft sizes.

## Research Question 2: What is the effect of varying the grafting wax composition on the rate of callus formation?

Expected Outcomes:  We hypothesize that the inclusion of calcium chloride in the grafting wax will stimulate a faster and more robust callus formation. We also predict that the standard commercial grafting wax will show an intermediate level of callus formation, while a control group without any added ingredients would demonstrate the poorest callus formation.  This experiment will provide insight into the potential role of specific additives in promoting vascular integration during crust grafting.
