## Course Description

Comprehensive introduction covering fundamental concepts and principles of Biology



## Module 1: Cellular and Molecular Biology

**Sessions**: 2

### Session 1: Cell Structure & Function

**Subtopics:**
- Prokaryotic vs. Eukaryotic Cells
- Cell Organelles (overview)
- Cell Membrane Structure & Transport
- Cell Wall Composition

**Learning Objectives:**
1. Identify key differences between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells.
2. Describe the function of major cell organelles.
3. Explain the mechanisms of passive and active transport.

**Key Concepts:**
- Cell theory
- Membrane potential
- Osmosis

**Rationale:** Establishes foundational cell biology concepts.

---

### Session 2: DNA & Protein Structure

**Subtopics:**
- DNA Structure & Replication
- Protein Structure (primary, secondary, tertiary)
- Central Dogma of Molecular Biology

**Learning Objectives:**
1. Describe the structure of DNA.
2. Explain the concept of protein structure.
3. Outline the flow of genetic information.

**Key Concepts:**
- Base pairing
- Amino acids
- Transcription & Translation

**Rationale:** Introduces core molecular biological processes.

## Module 2: Organisms and Processes

**Sessions**: 2

### Session 3: Cellular Respiration & Photosynthesis

**Subtopics:**
- Glycolysis
- Krebs Cycle
- Electron Transport Chain
- Photosynthesis (light & dark reactions)

**Learning Objectives:**
1. Describe the steps of cellular respiration.
2. Explain the process of photosynthesis.
3. Relate energy flow to metabolic pathways

**Key Concepts:**
- ATP production
- Redox reactions
- Light absorption

**Rationale:** Covers essential energy transformation processes.

---

### Session 4: Homeostasis & Biodiversity

**Subtopics:**
- Feedback Mechanisms
- Ecological Interactions (symbiosis, competition)
- Basic Biodiversity Concepts

**Learning Objectives:**
1. Explain the concept of homeostasis.
2. Describe ecological relationships.
3. Understand the diversity of life

**Key Concepts:**
- Thermoregulation
- Population dynamics
- Species interactions

**Rationale:** Introduces biological regulation and diverse life forms.
