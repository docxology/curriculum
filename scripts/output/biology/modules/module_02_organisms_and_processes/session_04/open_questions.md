

## Open Question 1: What is the mechanistic role of gut microbial metabolites in modulating host immune responses to environmental pollutants?

Context: Research increasingly highlights the crucial role of the gut microbiome in health and disease. However, the specific ways in which microbial metabolites – such as short-chain fatty acids – influence immune responses to external stressors like heavy metals or pesticides remain poorly understood. Identifying these mechanisms is critical for developing targeted interventions. Current research utilizes metagenomics, metabolomics, and animal models to investigate this complex interplay.

## Open Question 2: How does the dynamics of altered phenological timing in migratory bird populations directly impact the stability of interspecific trophic interactions within temperate forest ecosystems?

Context: Climate change is causing significant shifts in the timing of seasonal events, including migration patterns in many bird species. Understanding how these “phenological mismatches” disrupt established predator-prey relationships, particularly within complex, multi-trophic food webs, is a major priority. Research is employing network analysis, species distribution modelling, and long-term ecological monitoring to assess these cascading effects.

## Open Question 3: What are the implications of CRISPR-mediated gene editing in coral populations for enhancing their resilience to ocean acidification and warming temperatures, considering potential unintended consequences on coral community structure?

Context:  Coral reefs are facing unprecedented threats from climate change. CRISPR-Cas9 technology offers a potential tool for enhancing coral adaptation. However, the potential for off-target effects, unforeseen evolutionary responses, and the disruption of existing ecological relationships within reef ecosystems require careful investigation. Current research employs synthetic biology, ecological modelling, and laboratory experiments to evaluate the feasibility and risks of this approach.
